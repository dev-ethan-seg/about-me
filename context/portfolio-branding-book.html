01 — Identity

Brand Identity &
Positioning

A personal brand is the clearest signal you can give to the people who matter. This section defines what Ethan Segovia stands for, where he fits in the market, and what makes his perspective worth seeking out.

Positioning Statement

I build systems that work — and teams that understand why. A tech lead who operates at the intersection of engineering, product, and consulting, with a sharp eye for the financial and human implications of every decision.

Brand Pillars

Precision

Careful by default, not by accident. Whether it's a NetSuite SuiteQL query or a client email, details are non-negotiable. Work that's exact earns trust faster than work that's merely impressive.

Clarity

The ability to decode complex systems — ERP, data, finance — and explain them without condescension. This is the rarest skill in a hybrid technical-consulting role and the most valuable one.

Momentum

Bias toward shipping. A good solution in users' hands beats a perfect solution on a Jira board. Learner, builder, deployer — in that order, as fast as the problem allows.

Curiosity

Fintech, investing, ERP, front-end craft — the interests aren't scattered, they're connected. Systems thinker with range. Someone who reads the BSP's policy memo and the React changelog the same week.

Target Audience

Primary

Tech-forward companies

Fintech startups, ERP-dependent mid-market firms, and software-led consulting houses in PH/SEA looking for senior technical leadership.

Secondary

Collaborators & peers

Fellow engineers and PMs who might pull me into a project, refer me, or co-found something. Network-building audience.

Tertiary

Local dev community

Cebu/PH tech scene — building reputation as someone who shares knowledge and raises the floor.

Unique Value Proposition
①

Technical depth without tunnel vision. Writes code, reads balance sheets, runs client workshops. The rare engineer who doesn't need a translator.

②

Systems experience across the stack. From T3/Next.js front-ends to NetSuite SuiteQL, AWS infrastructure to Elixir/Phoenix backends — and the judgment to know when to use which.

③

Fintech-relevant financial literacy. Actively invests in PH stocks, REITs, and US equity funds. Understands the products he'd help build, not just the code that runs them.

Construction & Clearspace
ES
Clearspace = 10%

Always maintain a minimum clearspace equal to 10% of the mark's height on all sides. Never place other typographic elements within this zone. The italic ES is the anchor — it precedes any wordmark arrangement.

Do

  • Use DM Serif Display Italic for the monogram mark
  • Lead with accent olive on light backgrounds
  • Use muted olive (#a8c06a) on dark backgrounds
  • Pair monogram with small-caps tagline below
  • Allow wordmark-only version for small contexts

Don't

  • Stretch or distort the letterforms
  • Place on busy photographic backgrounds
  • Use the monogram in a non-italic weight
  • Apply drop shadows or outline effects
  • Swap to a different typeface for the mark

05 — Voice & Tone

How Ethan Sounds

Consistency in voice is what makes a brand feel like a person. These principles apply across the portfolio site, LinkedIn, project write-ups, and any public-facing writing.

Direct, not terse

Make the point clearly and early. No preamble. But don't truncate ideas that need room — be complete when it matters.

Precise, not cold

Technical accuracy is non-negotiable, but the human stakes always come first. Write for someone who needs to understand, not just someone who needs to be impressed.

Confident, not arrogant

Have opinions. Defend them. But acknowledge the edges of your knowledge honestly — that's what makes the strong claims credible.

Grounded in Cebu

Don't pretend to be from Silicon Valley. The Philippine tech context — BSP regulations, SME constraints, local market dynamics — is a feature, not a limitation.

06 — Imagery

Visual Language

The imagery strategy is editorial-minimal — no stock photos of offices or hands-on-laptop. The site's visuals come from the work itself: data, architecture diagrams, code, and the built product.

Visual Themes
⌨ Code as craft
◫ System architecture diagrams
▦ Data tables & dashboards
◈ Product UI screenshots
◌ Geometric layout motifs
≡ Typography as texture
Photography Direction (if used)
Context over aesthetic. Any photography should show the work or the environment — Cebu city context, whiteboard sessions, code on a real monitor. No posed professional headshots with generic blurred backgrounds.
Color treatment. If editing photos, desaturate slightly toward the paper palette. Cool highlights, warm shadows. Avoid Instagram-style heavy presets.
Aspect ratios. 16:9 for project banners, 1:1 for avatar/mark, 3:2 for editorial photos. Always crop with intentionality — no random square crops of landscape scenes.
